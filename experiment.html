Experiment

Details of the experiment conducted for the piezoelectric cells project.

Objective

This investigation explores how varying mechanical stress, such as applied weight, impacts the amount of electrical output of piezoelectric cells and explores practical ways to utilise energy that is already generated in society.

Materials Required

  • Piezoelectric cells (diameter of 29mm)
  • Weights (e.g., 20g,40g ,60g,80g, etc.)
  • Digital multimeter (to measure voltage)
  • Connecting wires
  • Soldering wire
  • Soldering iron

Experiment Procedure

  1. Set up the piezoelectric cells on a stable surface.
  2. Solder the wires onto the piezoelectric cells. One wire is on the positive part, and one is on the negative.
  3. Connect the other end of both wires to the multimeter for voltage measurement
  4. Apply the range of weights on the cells ranging from 20 to 200 grams and observe the voltage generated using the multimeter.
  5. Record the voltage output for different weights, starting from a low weight to a higher one.
  6. Apply the same amount of force while placing the weights on the cells and place the weights for the same amount of time (for less than a second) on the cells.
  7. Take each measurement 5 times
  8. Calculate the average energy generated (in volts)

Data Collection

Hypothesis: When mechanical stress applied increases, energy generated (in volts) also increases.

Variables:
Control Variable: Piezoelectric cells, the unit of multimeter, force at which the weight was applied, time for which the weight was applied.
Independent variable: amount of load or mechanical stress applied (in grams)
Dependent variable: electricity generated (in volts)

Record the voltage generated for different weight and impact conditions. Create a table to organize the results, making it easier to analyze the efficiency of the piezoelectric cells under different mechanical stresses.

Safety procedure:
Be careful when using the soldering iron, make sure it's done under adult supervision
Avoid keeping any liquid near all the electric equipment
